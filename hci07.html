Taking advantage of the Leap SDK Package in Unity and the IBM Watson voice recognition package, I built an interactive film system combining narration of a traditional film(external) and multimodal interaction events of users(internal). The system checks and proves users' ability to get involved in a virtual story with their hand gestures and voice. This project designed six types of hand gestures from the left and right hands, such as pointing, extending fingers, directing palm, grabbing, Etc., and also included a voice recognition system allowing a simple dialog between people and the film. I evaluated user experiences on overall perfomance(responding time and accuracy of recognition) and usability with 10 peers. Especially, the system worked well on simple(e.g., point) and complex gestures (e.g., point and grasp). Initially, hand gesture recognition can sometimes be too sensitive and activate unwanted events unintentionally; however, the system optimized the performance by designing and considering human interaction habits to avoid error.
